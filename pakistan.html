2024 Pakistani General Election

Geographical Spread

Pakistan's general election covered all four provinces - Punjab, Sindh, Khyber Pakhtunkhwa, and Balochistan - as well as Islamabad and the tribal areas, representing a diverse range of ethnic and cultural demographics.

Overview

The 2024 Pakistani General Election was a pivotal event in the nationâ€™s political trajectory. Held on February 8, 2024, it came after a period of political unrest following the removal of former Prime Minister Imran Khan. Approximately 60 million voters participated, marking a notable increase in registered voters compared to 2018. However, voter turnout dropped to 47.6%, reflecting public fatigue and a complex political environment.

Results

The election resulted in a fragmented National Assembly, with independent candidates, largely backed by PTI, securing 101 seats. Major party outcomes included:

  • Pakistan Muslim League-Nawaz (PML-N): 75 seats
  • Pakistan Peoples Party (PPP): 54 seats
  • Muttahida Qaumi Movement-Pakistan (MQM-P): 17 seats

Shehbaz Sharif of PML-N was re-elected as Prime Minister on March 3, 2024, following coalition negotiations.

Key Players

  • PML-N: Led by Shehbaz Sharif, the party aimed to maintain its influence amid economic difficulties.
  • PPP: Under Bilawal Bhutto Zardari, the party focused on expanding its influence beyond Sindh.
  • PTI: Despite Imran Khan's legal troubles and incarceration, the party managed to back a significant number of independent candidates.

Notable Issues

The election faced several challenges and controversies:

  • Allegations of Rigging: PTI claimed that results in at least 18 constituencies were manipulated, sparking legal battles.
  • Security Concerns: Balochistan witnessed coordinated attacks during the post-election period, reflecting ongoing regional tensions.
  • Economic Challenges: The political instability further strained Pakistan's economy, prompting an IMF bailout and rising inflation concerns.
  • Judicial and Legal Reforms: The ruling coalition passed laws curbing judiciary powers, drawing criticisms of undermining democracy.

Aftermath and Implications

The aftermath of the 2024 election highlighted the ongoing political fragmentation in Pakistan. The coalition government faced challenges in stabilizing the economy, navigating judicial disputes, and addressing security issues, while Imran Khan's incarceration continued to loom over the political landscape.
