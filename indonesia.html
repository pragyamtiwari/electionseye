2024 Indonesian General Election

Geographical Spread

The general election spans across Indonesia’s vast archipelago, encompassing over 17,000 islands and covering regions with diverse populations, cultures, and logistical challenges. The elections involve the selection of the President, Vice President, members of the People’s Consultative Assembly (MPR), and local legislative councils.

Overview

The 2024 Indonesian General Election is one of the world’s largest democratic exercises. It saw over 200 million registered voters participate, marking a significant display of civic engagement. Conducted on February 14, this election was pivotal in shaping Indonesia’s political trajectory, balancing its growing economic role in Southeast Asia with pressing domestic challenges. It involved simultaneous elections for both the executive and legislative branches, fostering heated debates and active political campaigns across the nation.

Results

The election saw a highly competitive race for the presidency, with Ganjar Pranowo representing the Indonesian Democratic Party of Struggle (PDI-P) and Prabowo Subianto of the Gerindra Party as leading candidates. Ganjar's platform focused on economic development and social reforms, while Prabowo emphasized security, national defense, and traditional values. Parliamentary results showcased a fragmented legislature, with significant gains for smaller parties, reflecting Indonesia’s political diversity and the role of coalition-building in governance.

Key Players

  • Ganjar Pranowo (PDI-P): Advocated for economic innovation, human resource development, and infrastructural projects.
  • Prabowo Subianto (Gerindra): Focused on national security, military strengthening, and sovereignty.
  • Anies Baswedan: Backed by a coalition of parties, championing social justice, pluralism, and anti-corruption efforts.

Notable Issues

The election revolved around several pressing issues that reflect Indonesia's domestic and regional challenges:

  • Economic Recovery: In the wake of the global economic downturn and pandemic impacts, candidates emphasized strategies to boost Indonesia’s economic resilience.
  • Corruption: Corruption remains a deep-seated issue, with candidates proposing various anti-graft measures to reform government practices and win voter confidence.
  • Environmental Policies: Given Indonesia’s rich biodiversity and environmental challenges, including deforestation and pollution, candidates presented varied approaches to sustainable development.
  • Identity Politics and Pluralism: Social cohesion and religious tolerance were highlighted, with several debates reflecting the nation's complex religious and ethnic landscape.
