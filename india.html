2024 Indian General Election

Geographical Spread

India's general election spans 28 states and 8 union territories, representing diverse demographics and regions.

Overview

The 2024 Indian General Election was a landmark event in the world’s largest democracy. Conducted across seven phases from April 19 to June 1, the election determined the members of the 18th Lok Sabha (House of the People), India’s lower house of Parliament. Approximately 642 million voters participated, with notable increases in women voter turnout.

Results

The election culminated in a win for the ruling Bharatiya Janata Party (BJP)-led National Democratic Alliance (NDA), which secured 293 seats but lost its single-party majority, with BJP winning 240 seats. The opposition coalition, INDIA, led by the Indian National Congress (INC), achieved significant gains, capturing 234 seats, with the Congress alone winning 99 seats.

Key Players

  • BJP: Led by Prime Minister Narendra Modi, BJP sought a third term.
  • INDIA Coalition: Formed in 2023, this alliance brought together the INC and regional allies under Rahul Gandhi’s leadership.
  • Congress: Winning 99 seats, Congress is now the official opposition after a decade.

Notable Issues

The election brought forth several notable issues:

  • Turnout: Voter turnout peaked at 69.16% in Phase 4 but showed variation across phases.
  • Electronic Voting Machines (EVMs): Instances of EVM malfunctions were reported, raising some concerns.
  • Political Climate: Issues like hate speech and political suppression were highlighted during campaigns.
