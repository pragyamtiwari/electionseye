2024 European Parliament Election

Geographical Spread

The European Parliament election covers all 27 member states, with each country electing Members of the European Parliament (MEPs) through proportional representation.

Overview

The 2024 European Parliament Election was a pivotal event in shaping the European Union’s legislative direction. Conducted from June 6 to June 9, it saw participation from over 200 million eligible voters across the EU, with notable increases in youth engagement and voter turnout in Central and Eastern Europe.

Results

The election resulted in a fragmented Parliament, with the European People's Party (EPP) remaining the largest political group, securing 170 seats, though losing seats compared to 2019. The Progressive Alliance of Socialists and Democrats (S&D) came in second with 145 seats. Green and liberal alliances, including Renew Europe, made gains, reflecting growing environmental and progressive concerns.

Key Players

  • EPP: Led by Manfred Weber, focusing on economic stability and EU unity.
  • S&D: Advocated for social reforms, led by Iratxe García.
  • Renew Europe: Led by Stéphane Séjourné, made gains emphasizing digital transformation and climate policies.

Notable Issues

The election highlighted several key issues:

  • Turnout: Voter turnout varied widely, with significant increases in several Eastern European countries.
  • Climate Change: Green policies and climate targets were a major point of debate.
  • Populism vs. Pro-EU Sentiments: The rise of populist and nationalist parties posed challenges to EU integration.
